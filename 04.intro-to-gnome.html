GNOME (pronounced ɡˈnoʊm or noʊm) is a desktop environment that is composed entirely of free and open-source software. GNOME was originally an acronym for GNU Network Object Model Environment. Its target operating system is Linux, but it is also supported on most derivatives of BSD. Source: Wikipedia

The "Super Key"

It wouldn't seem right to call the Windows key something that referred to a operating system and brand you are not using, so in GNOME we have the Super key. While most keyboards have a left and right Super key, it is the right Super that is used to switch between the overview and desktop. This can be changed to the right Supper using the Tweak Tool which you will be introduced to below.

Lock Screen

To move from the lock screen to the login screen press Space or Enter.

Desktop

As mentioned, there isn't much to see on the Desktop. The GNOME desktop is rather stark. This suits keeps the focus on the applications/windows you are working in. GNOME is also customizable view GNOME Shell Extensions With shell extensions, you can introduce many elements that may be familiar to you from other operating systems.

Top Bar

There are 3 main areas of the top bar.

  1. Perssing the button on the left side of the top bar brings-up the Overview which is described below.
  2. In the middle of the top bar is the day and time. Clicking on it brings-up the notification area and a calendar. Many applications post notivications here including calendar appointments.

    On the right side of the top bar is a group of icons for network, sound and power. Clicking on any of them brings-up the system menu which we look at later.

Overview

Pressing the Super key brings up the Overview

The vertical bar on the left is called the Dash and is used to launch applications. Clicking the grid button brings up installed applications. There are two buttons at the bottom of the page and . You can navigate through the pages of applications via keyboard or mouse.

On the left-side are the workspaces. When you first bring-up the overview they appear as a narrow vertical bar. To expand the bar as shown in the picture, move you mouse to the left side of the screen.

When the overview is active typing will begin a search which will show installed applications as well as applications available in the Software store.

Alt-Tab

Pressing Alt-Tab brings-up the application switcher. For applications that have more than one windows open pressing Alt-~ cycles through its windows.
