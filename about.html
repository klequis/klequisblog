This blog is inspired by my participation in Free Code Camp. I began the Free Code Camp course several months ago and spent a couple of weeks fully focused on it, sitting in my office (aka garage) by myself and interacting online. Learning always goes better when you have people to learn with so I started a Free Code Camp study group. The group has been active for only 10 weeks but has progressed from 1 meeting per week to two.

Without exception the people who come to the study group want a job, a different job, a better job and of couse a better paying job. The attendees are smart and dedicated. They are managing work and family while working to improve their lives.

You can find our group on Facebook Free Code Camp ... ,,, and on Meetup Tri-Valley / East Bay Free Code Camp Study Group.

Target Audiance

This blog is geared toward those learning to code. The posts are written with the assumption the reader has basic computer skills but no technical background. I have two prototype readers in mind as I write: 1) Person A: Has used a computer for work. They are comfortable using productivity applications such as a word processor and spreasheet, navigating and using the internet and installing software on a Windows or Mac. Person a has never coded and does not understand what is happening under the surface. 2) Person B: Is an experienced graphics designer. They have designed web sites, know HTML & CSS and are highly proficient at using complex software such as Adobe Photoshop. They may have dabbled in JavaScript but have never mastered any programming language. I am striving to write on a level that will help these readers, but it has been a couple of decatdes since I was one of them so I'm left wondering about the ways I no longer understand their needs. I welcome comments on this page regarding how I can better speak to these readers.
